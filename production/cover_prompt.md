# 表紙プロンプト

High-impact black-and-white theatrical release poster style anime / manga key visual for "Last Lie: MAI", using the attached character reference images as strict visual canon.

Match the reference images exactly: MAI's face, bob hair, ring-shaped eyes, android seams and education android outfit; Akari's face, hair and casual outfit; GRAVE's black armored silhouette and red mono-eye. Do not redesign any character.

Core concept: This is not a street-style character visual and not a simple lineup. It is a serious near-future school science fiction drama about a lie told to protect someone. The image should feel like a dramatic theatrical anime movie poster released in cinemas: powerful, emotional, elegant, memorable, and story-driven. It may have visual force and danger, but it must still prioritize MAI's quiet expression and the promise at the center of the story.

Central composition: Create a bold Japanese theatrical poster composition with clean readable silhouettes and no awkward overlapping limbs. MAI must appear exactly once in the entire image. Use one single three-quarter view of MAI standing in the center of a destroyed school corridor, looking forward calmly with ring-shaped eyes and empty hands relaxed at her sides. Use a slightly low camera angle so MAI feels iconic and still, while the environment around her feels dangerous. Place Akari in the lower-left foreground, smaller, partially back-view or profile, looking toward MAI with trust and fear mixed together, but do not make Akari touch MAI's body. Place GRAVE as a gigantic black armored mass looming behind MAI, larger than before, with the red mono-eye as the only color accent if color is used at all. GRAVE's silhouette should nearly fill the upper background and create strong pressure, but MAI must remain the emotional center.

Theatrical poster layout: Use a striking cinematic composition with strong diagonal rubble shapes, deep blacks, bright white rim light, sharp dust particles, dramatic smoke, and a large readable title area. Make the image feel like a Japanese cinema key visual for a science fiction drama with real scale and danger. Prioritize MAI's calm face, Akari's vulnerability, GRAVE's overwhelming shadow, and the emotional promise over literal combat.

Color and lighting: Black-and-white manga poster rendering, not full color. Use rich ink blacks, bright white rim light, strong screentone gradients, cinematic fog, and high contrast. A tiny red glow in GRAVE's mono-eye is acceptable as a single accent, but the overall poster should read as black-and-white. Avoid rainbow neon, over-saturated cyberpunk colors, soft pastel color grading, and flat low-contrast rendering.

Visual motifs: broken school corridor ceiling, fractured walls, falling debris, dust, speed-line-like rain of rubble, soft white glow around MAI's eyes, black shadow of GRAVE in the background, and heavy empty darkness around the title. Do not include city streets, fashion-poster posing, smiling idol poses, cyberpunk neon, superhero composition, chaotic battle combat, explosions covering the characters, ID cards, security tags, medals, keys, pendants, random handheld props, touching hands, awkward hands, extra fingers, hands placed on another character's waist or skirt, duplicate MAI, multiple MAI figures, large MAI face plus small MAI body, reflections of MAI, or any second depiction of MAI.

Keep all characters visually distinct and exactly matched to their own reference sheets.

Render the following cover text clearly in Japanese:
Title: ラスト・ライ
Subtitle: MAI
Tagline: その嘘は、世界でいちばん優しかった。

Text layout: Place the title in a large dramatic Japanese movie-poster style, with the subtitle smaller beneath it. Place the tagline as a small vertical or horizontal line with enough breathing room. Text must be readable and must not cover the characters' faces.

Do not add any other text, logo, watermark, signature, fake credits, production company names, release dates, or English taglines.
