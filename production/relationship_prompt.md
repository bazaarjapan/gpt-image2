# キャラクター相関図プロンプト

Create one vertical black-and-white Japanese manga character relationship chart page for the manga "ラスト・ライ / MAI".

Use the attached character reference images as strict visual canon. Match each character exactly to their reference image. Do not swap faces, hairstyles, outfits, android seams, or GRAVE armor.

Page purpose:
This page goes between the cover and P01. It should feel like a theatrical manga booklet character relationship page. The top priority is clarity: a reader should understand the five characters and their relationships in three seconds.

Layout:
- Vertical 1024x1536 manga infographic page.
- Use a clean relationship-map layout, not a dramatic full-page illustration.
- Put MAI in the center in the largest framed portrait box.
- Put 朝倉 灯里 in the lower left framed portrait box.
- Put 朝倉 透也 in the upper left framed portrait box.
- Put GRAVE in the upper right framed portrait box as a dark armored bust or upper body.
- Put ECHO in the lower right framed portrait box.
- Keep each character inside their own rectangular frame or clean label area.
- Use thick simple arrows and short labels between boxes.
- Leave enough white space around every arrow label.
- Use large readable Japanese typography.
- Do not overlap character art with relationship labels.
- Do not make characters full-body if it makes the chart crowded; bust or half-body portraits are preferred.

Visual tone:
Japanese theatrical manga booklet infographic design, black-and-white, clean ink, subtle screentone, clear boxes, readable labels, elegant arrows, strong visual hierarchy, no random text, no watermark, no signature.

Important text rules:
- Use only the Japanese labels listed above.
- Do not invent extra text.
- Do not use speaker balloons. This is a relationship chart, so use labels, arrows, and short captions.
- Keep all text large enough to read.
- Avoid decorative tiny text.
- Avoid cluttered backgrounds behind text.
- Avoid duplicate characters.
- Avoid changing MAI into "マアイ"; the label must be "MAI".
